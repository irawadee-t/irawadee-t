My Work

Neural Engineering

“What if electrical connections between the brain and the stomach can tell us about diseases such as Parkinson’s and eating disorders?”

To answer that question, I'm currently doing neural engineering research at Stanford; I coded Coleman lab's first experimental interface using the PsychoPy framework for simultaneous gut-brain signal recording and assembled hardware (Mentalab and OpenBCI) to record gut and brain electrical signals with the end goal of classifying Parkinson's disease subtypes.

Social Innovation & Entrepreneurship

From ideation to now being acquired - I built Sandee for Good - an online donating platform that connects donors to rural nonprofits across all 5 regions of Thailand and has been featured on various global media and news channels such as CNN & UNICEF. I’ve also prototyped a Low-Cost Ventilator for rural hospitals and won the Thailand Innovation Awards, along with Second place at Taiwan International Science Fair.
